On-Duty Engineering

The AI Platform team ran an on-duty rotation — each engineer took turns as the first responder for alerts, incoming pings, and stakeholder queries. The role was as much about routing as fixing: knowing when something wasn't yours and making sure the right person got looped in without creating a bottleneck.

The OpenCV migration is a good illustration of why alerting matters more than perfect code. I had instrumented decode-failure metrics from day one — not because I expected something to go wrong, but because shipping without visibility is flying blind. When failures surfaced, the alert fired immediately and gave us a clear signal to investigate rather than waiting for user reports. The root cause turned out to be SVG files, which neither PIL nor OpenCV can decode — unrelated to the migration entirely. The fix was redirected to the right upstream service. Without the alerting, that incident could have gone undetected for much longer. The monitoring you write before something breaks is always worth more than the debugging you do after.
